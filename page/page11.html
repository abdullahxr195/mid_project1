Food

Ramen

The ramen consists of vermicelli and meat soup mixed with a variety of ingredients including tall seaweed, tuna chips, dried baby sardines, cow bones, shiitake mushrooms and onions.

MakiZushi

Sushi usually refers to a plate of rice pressed with vinegar with a piece of raw fish or shellfish, called nita, on top, and vegetables.These ingredients are wrapped together using dried seaweed leaves called nori, and sushi is generally eaten with soy sauce and wasabi

Tempura

A dish that includes ingredients such as seafood, meat and vegetables covered in the mixture and fried in oil. The mixture usually contains flour and eggs. Tempura is generally dipped in a special sauce called tentsuyu before eating

Yakitori

Yakitori refers to Japanese grilled chicken where pieces of chicken meat, each part of which is grilled with a specific type of skewer called koshi, and then grilled over a charcoal fire

Miso

A broth made from soy paste and fish broth (dashi broth), along with chives, tofu, and other ingredients

Shabu shabu

It consists of slices of delicious beef with vegetables added and boiled in water. It can also be served with dipping sauces in charming ceramic pots.

Yakiniku

These are small pieces of beef and vegetables that are cooked on the grill in front of you

Soba

Soba noodles are made from buckwheat flour, have a gray color and are served hot with broth or cold with dipping sauce

Teppanyaki

One of the most famous and popular Japanese foods in Japan, the teppanyaki cooking and preparation device is called teppan, and it comes with a flat metal surface. Teppan works similarly to a grill. You can cook anything from steak, chicken, seafood, vegetables, or even noodles on it

Onigiri

It consists of rice balls wrapped in nori paper and formed into triangles or cylinders

Coffee Jelly

Made with sweetened coffee and gelatin, it is cooled and then cut into small cubes, and usually served with whipped cream or condensed milk

Milky Custard

It is prepared from milk, sugar, eggs and vanilla, then cooked over low heat to obtain a smooth and velvety texture, and can be served warm or cold with caramel or fruit sauce

Japanese cheesecake

It is a light, crisp cheesecake known for its mouth-melting cotton texture. It is prepared with simple ingredients such as cream cheese, eggs, milk, and flour, with the whites whipped for a fluffy texture. It is baked in a water bath style to give it a smooth texture and light air

dorayaki

Consisting of two golden sponge pancakes that resemble pancakes, usually filled with sweet red bean paste (anko), it has a soft texture and delicious flavour, and can also be found with modern fillings such as custard or chocolate

taiyaki

It is a traditional Japanese dessert that comes in the form of a fish made of a paste similar to pancake batter and filled with sweet bean paste. There are also many alternative fillings such as custard cream, chocolate, or cheese

hotteok

It is a type of stuffed Korean pancake that is fried until golden brown, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. It is also usually stuffed with a mixture of brown sugar, cinnamon and nuts
