Food

Indian biryani

Fry the onions, add the garlic and ginger and stir well.Then we put the chicken pieces on them, add the Indian spices and salt and stir

Curry

Ingredients range from lamb and chicken to vegetables like okra or Indian cheese (paneer), and even a unique fruit like persimmons

Thali

The following usually includes slow-cooked dal, marinated vegetables, cold yogurt, spicy pickles, aromatic chatni, fresh bread (roti or puri), and steamed white rice

Tandoori Chicken

Season the tandoori chicken with yoghurt, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, and a variety of spices such as masala, paprika, and turmeric. Then grill in a clay oven

Chicken Tikka Masala

Season the boneless chicken pieces with a mixture of buttermilk and aromatic spices, then grill them over a fire and cook them in a rich, creamy tomato sauce

Rogan Josh

It is a strong-flavored lamb dish characterized by its dark red color and rich aroma. The meat is slowly cooked in a mixture of buttermilk, caramelized onions, garlic and Kashmiri red pepper

Malai Kofta

Malay kofta is the vegan answer to meatballs, with a rich, creamy taste. “Kofta” Fried balls of mashed potatoes, paneer (Indian cheese), nuts, and raisins

Butter Chicken

The chicken is prepared using boneless pieces, seasoned for hours in a mixture of buttermilk, garlic, ginger, lemon juice, and spices such as gram masala, red pepper powder, and cumin

Vindaloo

It used to contain pork, vinegar, garlic, and wine, but local chefs replaced the wine with palm vinegar and introduced a variety of Indian spices such as red chili pepper, cumin, cinnamon, and sometimes cardamom and cloves. The result: curry that is spicy, sour, aromatic, and very bold

Palak Paneer

Fresh mashed spinach is cooked with light spices, garlic, ginger, and pieces of soft Indian cheese (banir)

Dosa

Dosa is a thin, nutritious crepe made from fermented rice and a mixture of urad dal (black lentils). It is golden-brown in color and slightly sour in flavor. It is cooked on a hot plate until crispy, often wrapped around a spicy, seasoned potato filling

Gulab Jamun

Gulab jamun are golden or brown balls, prepared from khoya (solid milk) and fried until crispy on the outside, then dipped in an aromatic syrup of sugar, rose water, cardamom, and saffron. Whether served hot or cold

Dosa

Dosa is a thin, nutritious crepe made from fermented rice and a mixture of urad dal (black lentils). It has a golden-brown color and a slightly sour flavor, is cooked on a hot plate until crispy, and is often wrapped around a seasoned and spicy potato filling.

Lassi

It is prepared by mixing yogurt with water and sometimes milk, and can be sweet, salty, or even spicy. Sweet lassi may contain sugar, rose water, cardamom or mango pulp, while salty lassi may include cumin, black salt, and mint.

Falooda

It starts with rose syrup, then soaked vermicelli, sweet basil seeds (sabja), and cold milk, followed by a ball of ice cream, sometimes garnished with nuts or fruits. Cubes of jelly or kulfi (Indian ice cream) can be added to it

Crepes are one of the most famous French dishes that are popular worldwide. Crepes are characterized by their delicacy and delicious taste, and can be eaten sweet with chocolate, jam, or sugar, or salty with cheese and meat. Crepes are the perfect breakfast or dinner snack and are an essential part of traditional French cuisine.
