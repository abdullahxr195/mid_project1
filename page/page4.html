Food

paella

Paella may be the most famous Spanish dish of all time, and without a doubt one of the most distorted. Paella is native to the Valencia region, and there are two types: Paella Valenciana, which is prepared with rabbit and chicken meat; and Paella seafood. The saffron component gives rice its distinctive color

patatas bravas

It is prepared by cutting potatoes into cubes, then lightly frying them, and serving the same method everywhere. The sauce varies between a spicy ketchup sauce, garlic mayonnaise with a sprinkle of smoked pepper (pimento), or a combination of the two

Gazpacho soup

Made with tomatoes, it is particularly famous for being served cold. This may shock some, but in Seville's sweltering summer heat, its appeal becomes clear. Its main ingredients, in addition to tomatoes, are pepper, garlic, pieces of bread, and large amounts of olive oil.

Fideuà dish

Fideuà is a type of Spanish pasta that resembles fine vermicelli. It is popular in Catalonia and Valencia and is used in seafood dishes that rival paella in flavor and complexity.

Spanish omelet dish

Chorizo sausages, peppers and onions, among other ingredients, but culinary experts recommend containing only potatoes and eggs. Cut the potatoes into cubes and fry them slightly before adding them to the egg mixture and frying them over high heat. The most difficult part is turning the pan to turn the omelet.

Albondigas

Albondigas, or meatballs with tomato sauce, are a classic snack of Spanish cuisine and are served throughout Spain. There is a delicious type of this dish in which meatballs are served covered with almond sauce, without tomatoes

Bakalao fish dish

Bacalao, or salted cod, is a valuable dish in Spain, brought by Spanish fishermen over hundreds of years from as far away as Norway. You don't find this fish in local waters, so it was salted to preserve it during the trip

Castilian soup

Ingredients: water, eggs, bread, and garlic, sometimes with black pudding or chorizo added

Gazpacho Andalusia and Salmorejo Cordobí

The main ingredient is tomatoes. Gazpacho is prepared from tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, vinegar and oil, and several pieces of chopped cucumbers or red and green peppers are added to it to serve in the dish. As for the salmorkho dish, tomatoes, bread, oil and garlic are added to it, as well as boiled eggs, pork and pieces of stale bread

Madrid Stew

The first dish is with soup, and the second dish is with vegetables, legumes and pieces of meat. Cook all ingredients together in a pressure cooker with water. This recipe consists of vegetables such as carrots, cabbage, and kale. Legumes are usually chickpeas, and beef, pork, or chicken can be used. When cooked together, this recipe gains flavor

churro

Churros are a popular snack made from fried dough, cut into sausage shapes and dipped in sugar.

Toruón - Spanish almond dessert

TURNON is a very old traditional dessert of Maghrebi (Arab) origin. This Spanish Nougat almond is a traditional Christmas treat in Spain. The original toron is mainly found in almonds and honey, but today it comes in a wide range of flavours.

Polvorones

"Rich and crumbly polvorones" are a delicious cookie, especially popular during the Christmas season. The name comes from the word "polvo", meaning dust. You may think it's strange, but it's so delicate it looks dusty and should be handled with caution. They are very rich and practically melt in your mouth!

Flan - Custard with caramel sauce and more

Classic flannel is flannel custard, topped with caramel sauce. It is one of the most popular desserts, served everywhere in Spain. Vanilla is a classic or traditional flavor.

Mantecados

They are really my favorites. No wonder - it's so soft, it melts in your mouth. Light, with its delicate anise flavor, and words don't do them justice. You just have to make them understand why they are Spanish favorites, and why there is no Spanish home without "mantecados" at Christmas!
